Signs you might recognise

Every autistic child is different. You may notice your child:

  • Communicates differently, with delayed, limited, or advanced but literal language
  • Finds comfort in routine, and real distress when it changes
  • Is sensitive to sounds, lights, textures or crowds
  • Finds eye contact, turn-taking or reading social cues hard
  • Has deep, focused interests they know everything about

Understanding it

A neuro-affirming view of autism

Autism is a difference in how a person experiences the world, communicates, and relates to others. It is not an illness to cure or a result of parenting. Autistic children are simply wired differently, and with understanding and the right support they can flourish. We take a neuro-affirming approach: we build on strengths and never try to make a child "less autistic".

Every autistic child is different. As the saying goes, if you have met one autistic child, you have met one autistic child. So we start by understanding your child, then shape support around them, not the other way around.

"I don't want him changed. I want him understood, and helped to cope with a world that isn't built for him." That is exactly the approach we take.

Common questions

What parents ask us about autism support

Do you try to "cure" or change my autistic child?

No. We are neuro-affirming. We never try to make a child less autistic. We build on their strengths and gently support communication, regulation and coping, so the world feels more manageable.

Do we need a diagnosis to start?

No diagnosis is required to begin. We can start with a screening and a settling-in period to understand your child, then shape support around them.

My child is non-speaking or minimally speaking. Can you help?

Yes. We meet children where they are and support all forms of communication, not just spoken words. Connection and being understood come first.

Is it one-to-one or in a group?

Both. We use small groups and one-to-one sessions depending on your child's needs and comfort, and we go at their pace.

Do you offer online support?

Yes, on a case-by-case basis, though many autistic children do better with the predictability of in-person sessions. We will advise honestly on what suits your child.

Where are you located?

We are based in Taipan, Subang Jaya, and support families across Petaling Jaya, the Klang Valley and Selangor, with online support available case by case.
